Welcome to VeriFast!

VeriFast is a scientific literature research assistant. You enter the name of the article that you want to read, we find it in the web and load it, you read it. The main feature of our assistant is an option of citation verification. How often do you find yourself in a situation when you get intersted (or suspicious) in some statement that cites another paper? What do you do? Presumably you find this paper and then scan it trying to figure out what exactly the authors meant by referencing this paper. The relevant info might be in the abstract, but it might as well be buried somewhere deep in the methods section. With our tool all you gotta do is to click on the citation, that's it! On the right panel the most relevant paragraphs of the cited article will appear - they most likely contain the information related to the sentence that contains the citation you were interested in. Just check them out and be done with it. If you're still not satisfyed, you can also open the cited article in new tab in one click.
